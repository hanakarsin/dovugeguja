ODM SG Edwin Sifuna on spot over earlier promise to resign if Thirdway Alliance got 1 million signat

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Tuesday, April 23, 2024

- Sifuna earlier claimed Thirdway Alliance lacked the technical and political following to necessitate the signature collection process

- The secretary general based his argument on the Opposition party's failure to raise the same in 2016 during Okoa Kenya campaign

- Kenyans mounted pressure on him to keep his promise and resign after the electoral body verified and approved the Thirdway Alliance signatures

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) Secretary General Edwin Sifuna's utterances over inability of Thirdway Alliance to raise one million signatures have come to haunt him.

Kenyans who have mastered the art of using the internet to retrieve archived confessions, trolled Sifuna immediately the electoral body announced the Ekuru Aukot-led outfit had met the one million threshold needed for a referendum.

In a past interview with KBC, the ODM man laughed off the quest by Aukot's party to collect the signatures saying it lacked both technical capacity and political following to necessitate such a course.

The vocal politician went ahead to state he would resign as the Orange party secretary general if Thirdway Alliance managed to collect a million signatures.

"Thirdway Alliance is a nuisance. I can guarantee you and I can bet here in front of this country that if Thirdway Alliance are able to get a million signatures, I will resign as secretary general of ODM. I know for a fact they can't," he said during the interview

Backing his argument on the fact that his own party in collaboration with other political outfits which formed the powerful Coalition for Restoration of Democracy (CORD) had failed to meet the threshold, he concluded Aukot's quest was a tall order.

" We did the Okoa Kenya initiative and I know what it takes to raise one million signatures, the people know where to look for leadership, I know who they are, and I know who they are not," he added

During the 2016 Okoa Kenya initiative, the electoral agency only found 891,598 signatures to be valid after the verification exercise.

The Opposition team then accused the commission of colluding with the government to frustrate its efforts to "redeem the country ".
