Kogi Governorship Election Declared Inconclusive

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Wednesday, August 7, 2024

Today, November 22, residents of Kogi state will know who will take charge of the state’s affairs for the next four years: Idris Wada of the Peoples Democratic Party or Audu Abubakar, who is the All Progressives Congress candidate.

Yesterday, November 21, the INEC shifted the collation exercise to this morning, as results did not trickle in from the local government areas.

16: 35 pm: The INEC declares Kogi governorship election inconclusive as the leading margin (between the two leading parties) is below the number of registered voters in cancelled polling units.

The supplementary election should be held on a date to be announced by the commission. The law states that supplementary election is to be conducted within one week as of today.
