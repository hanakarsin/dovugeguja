Business ideas in Nigeria: 50 workable opportunities in 2022

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Monday, January 1, 0001

Nigeria is among Africa's most vibrant economies. Still, the country is plagued by numerous problems, one of which is rampant unemployment. This has led countless people to pursue self-employment in the form of wide-ranging business ventures. Like in numerous other countries, some businesses perform better than others due to various factors. Which are the top business ideas in Nigeria today?

Whether you are looking for a full-time self-employment venture or a small-scale business in Nigeria to serve as a side hustle, there are numerous businesses with which you cannot go wrong.

The best business ideas in Nigeria in 2022

Here is a look at some great business opportunities in Nigeria.

1. Livestock farming

One of the fast business opportunities in Nigeria is livestock farming. However, this industry is yet to discover its fullest potential.

For this business, you will need some patience and knowledge and enlist the services of a livestock rearing specialist. Some of the niche areas that you can venture into include catfish farming, poultry farming, cattle grazing, goat rearing, and snail farming.

2. Blogging

It might not be an entirely new idea, but blogging has not been commercialized enough in Nigeria, as is the case in more developed countries. When implemented correctly, blogging can easily be one of the most profitable online businesses due to the numerous monetization options.

Becoming a blogger can be a good source of income from the comfort of your home. All one must do is find a niche and target the right audience.

3. Agricultural product exportation

Nigeria has a remarkably vibrant agriculture sector. You can start your own exportation business if you register with the Export Promotion Council of Nigeria and decide on the specific product you would want to export. Some of the products in high demand abroad include cassava flakes, cocoa, yam flour, groundnut, chilli peppers, cashew nuts, mango, and many others.

4. Health and fitness training

Health is something most people do not take for granted in the modern day. As a result, you can make money if you can turn health and fitness into a business. There are not so many well-equipped gyms, especially in small towns.

If you can get your hands on some capital, put in place a state-of-the-art gym, and your business will be a success story in no time. You will, however, have or hire someone who has the right skills to train people.

5. Mining

Are you looking for a lucrative business in Nigeria? Then you should consider venturing into the mining industry. Nigeria is endowed with numerous mineral resources. Some of the common ones include coal, iron ore, limestone, bitumen, and others. The Nigerian mining sector has constantly been growing, paving the way for new investments.

If you have access to some significant capital outlay, a skilled and knowledgeable team, and a ready market, you could venture into this lucrative industry.

If you have sufficient capital to start a private refinery, you will benefit greatly from the government's numerous subsidies and other monetary incentives.

7. Social media marketing

You probably spend a lot of time on Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, and other social media platforms. However, you may not know that you can earn while interacting with your buddies and fans online. Monetize your social media accounts by making them a platform for others to advertise their products.
