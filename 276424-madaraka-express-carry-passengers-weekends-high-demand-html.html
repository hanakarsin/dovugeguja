Madaraka Express to carry more passengers on weekends amid high demand

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, March 19, 2024

- High demand has seen many families and groups wait longer before they can actualise their weekend coast trips aboard the SGR

- Kenya Railways has increased the Madaraka Express coaches to 16 from 11 for economy class passengers on weekends

- Apart from the KSh1 billion the SGR raked in last year, it has created about 46,000 local jobs

- It has also reduced transportation cost and increased Kenya's gross domestic product by about 1.5%

Kenya Railways has finally yielded to public demand and increased the number of coaches on the standard gauge railway’s Madaraka Express trains between Nairobi and Mombasa over the weekends.

While the Madaraka Express has ferried over 1.3 million passengers in 1,142 trips since inception by the time it marked its first anniversary on May 31, 2018, there is no denying the fact that many travellers have at some point been left high and dry with notifications that the train was fully booked.

This is bearing in mind that many families, churches and even corporates, especially in Nairobi, are increasingly organising short trips to the coast, leaving the capital on Friday and returning on Sunday, well in time for work on Monday.

To address the deficit, Kenya Railways will starting this week increase the number of economy class passenger-coaches to 16 up from 11 on Fridays, Saturdays and Sundays.

The additional 590 seats means the trail will have the capacity to carry 1,888 in the economy class in one go. This is to add to the two 72-passengers coaches in the first class.

According to the corporation’s managing director Atanas Maina, they have been studying travel trends and noted the demand is always higher from Fridays.

Over the past one year, the Madaraka Express recorded an impressive 96.7% seat occupancy.

The SGR passenger train service has been offering a four-and-a-half-hour non-stop connection between the country’s two major cities, a massive improvement on the 10-hours it takes to cover the 472 kilometres by bus.

Kenya Railways currently offers two commuter train trips in each direction daily, up from one initially.

Passengers pay KSh 3,000 on first class coaches and KSh 1,000 on economy class coaches.

The inter-county service train that was launched December 2017 charges KSh 50 from Mombasa to Mariakani, KSh 230 from Voi to Kibwezi, KSh 130 from Athi River to Emali, KSh 510 from Nairobi to Voi and KSh400 from Emali to Maisenyi on economy class coaches.

On first class, passengers have been paying KSh 140 from Mombasa to Mariakani, KSh 920 from Voi to Kibwezi, KSh 660 from Athi River to Emali, KSh 2,130 from Nairobi to Voi and KSh 1,670 from Emali to Maisenyi.

The train has a capacity of 1,260 passengers with 15 economy class coaches and three first class coaches.

It also comes with a luggage carrier with a total capacity of 70 tonnes and a sleeper coach.
