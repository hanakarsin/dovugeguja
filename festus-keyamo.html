Festus Keyamo Breaks Silence On Bribing Charly Boy

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Thursday, June 6, 2024

The spokesman of the President Muhammadu Buhari Campaign Organisation, Festus Keyamo, has denied the claim that he paid an amount running into “nine figures” to Charles Oputa, popularly known as Charly Boy.

Naija News had reported earlier that Charly Boy made the allegation in a video interview, after an earlier claim by Deji Adeyanju, Convener of Concerned Nigerians, that he collected money from Keyamo.

However, in a bid to clarify the supposed circumstances under which he collected the money, Charly Boy, a convener of our “Mumu Don Do” movement, alleged that Keyamo paid the said amount to him for a song he composed against Atiku Abubakar, the presidential candidate of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), Nigeria’s main opposition party.

It is believed that the said amount was given to Charly Boy during the heat of the Presidential campaign.

Reacting to Charly Boy’s allegation in a post on the micro-blogging site, Twitter on Friday evening, Keyamo said anyone who believed he paid a penny for any song by the latter needs to have their heads examined.

In a series of tweets, the Senior Advocate of Nigeria wrote: “1. I’ve been having some good laugh. Whoever thinks that I would pay a penny for a song by @Areafada1 that disses both the APC and PDP must have his/her head examined. If the quarrelling parties want to hurt their egos by bandying phantom figures, they should leave me out of it.
