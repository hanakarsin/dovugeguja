Uhuru, Ruto lead Kenyans in wishing 2019 KCPE candidates success as national exams begin

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Wednesday, May 8, 2024

- Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) starts today, Monday, October 29

- Uhuru has exuded confidence the candidates will excel in the in the national exams

- He urged them to desist from examination malpractice and believe in what they have been taught

- Ruto said the country was relying on the candidates' success and banking on their future innovations

President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy William Ruto have led the country in wishing all the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) candidates success in their exams which commenced Tuesday, October 29, morning.

The leaders asked the candidates to desist from examination malpractices and believe in themselves and what they have been taught in class.

Uhuru who also extended his best wishes to Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) candidates urged the learners to take the tests as a step towards their dreams and aspirations adding he was confident they would excel.

"My prayers and best wishes to you all as you sit for your KCPE and KCSE. As I always tell you, remember exams are not the end but a step towards dreams and aspirations you all have for your future. All teachers, parents, guardians and by large all Kenyans are rooting for you in this phase of your lives," said the president

Uhuru added his government was committed to ensuring all candidates would have an opportunity to advance their education through High School, universities, colleges and Technical and Vocational Educational Trainings (TVETs).

"I believe in you and I know you have been imparted with adequate knowledge and information for the task ahead of you. Every effort and sacrifice has also been made to ensure you succeed," he added.

Ruto also wished the candidates all the best in the national test exuding confidence they would excel.

A total of 1,088,986 candidates registered for the 2019 KCPE test and are expected to sit for the final exam on Thursday, October 31.

The Teachers' Service Commission (TSC) licensed 28,000 school heads to act as centre managers during examinations while 173,000 teachers were assigned to officiate the examinations
