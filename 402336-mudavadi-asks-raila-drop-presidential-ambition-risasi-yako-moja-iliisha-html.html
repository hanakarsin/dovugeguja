Amani National Congress (ANC) party leader Musalia Mudavadi has asked Opposition leader Raila Odinga to forego his 2022 presidential ambitions and back him.

The ANC leader said Raila had already exhausted his trials and should live by his 2017 word where he insisted he was vying for the last time( firing his last bullet).

Mudavadi recalled how the ODM leader implored him together with the other then National Super Alliance (NASA) principals to endorse him as the outfits flag bearer in the polls as he was trying for the last time.

" I, Moses Wetangula, Kalonzo Musyoka and Isac Ruto fronted him in 2017 so that he can use his last bullet as he claimed, we told him to aim properly but unfortunately, he lost. We are now telling him to let us use our bullets," said Mudavadi.

The former vice-president who has already declared interest in succeeding President Uhuru Kenyatta said, unlike Raila, he still has more (bullets) chances to run for the top seat.

He, however, expressed reservations on Raila living by his 2017 word as he is seemingly still interested in the top seat.

Until BBI passes

Raila has been reluctant to publicly declare his presidential bid. However, he stated that the decision to vie for the presidency in 2022 will depend on the fate of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) bill.

The former premier said that the success of the BBI bill will play an integral role in his decision to give a stab at the presidency for the fifth time.

"Kabla BBI haijapita sisema kama nitasimama ama mimi sitasimama (Before BBI passes, I cannot announce whether I will vie or not) said Raila.

The former prime minister has been popularising the BBI document across the country since its official launch in October 2020.

In a separate story, political analyst Herman Manyora described Mudavadi as a safe pair of hands who could also land the presidency.
