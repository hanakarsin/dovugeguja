Homa Bay Governor Cyprian Awiti admitted at Nairobi Hospital weeks after returning from medical leav

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Thursday, July 18, 2024

- The Homa Bay governor was taken ill and admitted at Agha Khan Hospital on Thursday, June 27

- He was later transferred to Nairobi on Friday, June 28

- According to doctors, he is in stable condition

- His hospitalisation comes barely two weeks after he made first public appearance after coming from medical leave

- Awiti had gone to seek medical attention for his eye problem in Germany

Barely two weeks after Homa Bay Governor Cyprian Awiti resumed work, he has been taken ill again.

The county boss was taken ill on Thursday, June 27, while attending an official event within the county.

A report by Daily Nation indicated the governor was admitted at the Aga Khan Hospital in Kisumu.

Further reports indicate he was later transferred to the hospital's Nairobi branch on Friday, June 28, for further treatment.

Doctors said the county boss, who has an eye problem, is in a stable condition.

Awiti had been missing in action for about two months while seeking medical attention for his eye problem in Germany.

The county boss then made first public appearance on Tuesday, June 18, while touring the offices of the Homa Bay Water and Sanitation Company.

While cautioning his opponents, who according to him, have been undermining his development projects, Awiti noted he was looking forward to retire from politics.

According to him, some politicians were trying to make him look like a failure and destroying county projects to make Homa Bay look like an area without development.

He further explained that the destruction of projects would make him seem unable to achieve his goals and to leave the legacy he promised the people during campaigns.

“There are people using youths to cause the destruction of county projects. They do this to make me look like I have not done anything for my electorate. They will not succeed,” he said.
