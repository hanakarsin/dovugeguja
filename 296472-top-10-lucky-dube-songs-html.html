Top 10 Lucky Dube songs

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Tuesday, February 20, 2024

People in all corners of Africa and the world at large like listening to reggae music. One of the greatest pioneers of Reggae in Africa is believed to be the late Lucky Dube. He was a South African reggae musician and the most successful reggae artist. The icon, a multi-award-winning superstar of the genre, spoke not only of pain and suffering in his lyrics but also of unity, peace, freedom, and respect. Lucky Dube songs represented an era of revolution and a generation of ambitious youth that yearned for a better Africa, free from foreign influence. When he died the lyrics from his tracks became sacred, and his voice was therapy for many Africans.

Lucky Dube birth name is Lucky Philip Dube. Lucky Dube worked as a gardener when he was a child. As he grew up, he realized that he was not getting enough income to cater to his family and so he began to attend school. At school, he expressed his interest in music and joined a choir with some of his childhood friends. Together they formed the first musical group known as The Skyway Band.

He also started the Rastafarian movement while still at school. When he reached the age of 18, he joined his cousins’ band known as The Love Brothers. They played Zulu pop music popularly known as Mbaganga. To continue funding for his lifestyle he worked for Hole and Cooke as a security personnel. The band later signed with Teal Record Company. Though Dube was still at school, the band recorded material in Johannesburg during his school holidays. They released an album under Lucky Dubes’ name. Another album was later released and this time Dube wrote the lyrics in addition to singing. He began learning English at that point. Lucky Dube music were the most popular in the 90’s in the African continent.

Which are the best Lucky Dube songs?

The artist who left this world at the age of 43 during and after his time continues to bless us with his socio-political critical lyrics. This increased popularity of reggae music and spread faster to many parts of Africa. Many of reggae die-hards confess they listen to his music because of its message and because of their ability to relate to the message in the song.
