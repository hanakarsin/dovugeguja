3 witnesses fail to identify suspect found with KSh 1.9m narcotics on Tanzania bound bus

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Tuesday, July 23, 2024

- The three were employees of Tahmeed bus company that operates between Mombasa and Dar es Salaam

- They said they could not identify the suspect since police did not give them time to assess CCTV footage captured during their interaction with the accused

- The suspect was identified as Dennis Msando who had denied charges and was released on a bond of KSh 1 million with a surety of similar amount

Three workers of a Kenya-Tanzania bound bus failed to positively identify a suspect accused of trafficking heroine worth KSh 1.9 million.

The narcotics were confiscated by police officers at the Tahmeed bus termini in 2018 after being being sent from Tanzania as a parcel, labelled "document".

Tahmeed Mombasa Office Manager, cargo supervisor and an IT manager failed to identify the prime suspect in the crime after he was presented in court saying they could not recall his face.

Appearing before Mombasa Principal Magistrate Martin Rabera, the witnesses said they did not see the person who was supposed to receive the cargo since police had put them under pressure to produce the CCTV clips denying them time to assess the footage.

“Three people came to my office with a parcel number and introduced themselves as police officers. They demanded to see the document and since our bus had arrived from Dar es Salaam and delivered all the cargo, we showed themThey did not explain why they wanted to know the person who was to receive the parcel,” said Jamal Shamsa Abdallah, Tahmeed Coach Manager.

Shamsa further disclosed the same police officers returned to the office few days later and demanded to review a CCTV recording from the time the parcel arrived to when it was picked.

He directed the ICT manager to give the officers access to the recordings and was not informed about the developments.

The cargo supervisor, Mohamed Jera, who also testified told the court that the parcel was sent from Tanzania by a man identified as Charles Mringa and was supposed to be received by a person identified as Denis Msando in Mombasa.

He said there were a lot of parcels which were received the same day and he could not tell what each parcel contained adding company rules did not allow one to open any parcel from their clients.

He also denied knowing Musando stating he could not master the face of every client who comes to the office to pick items.

The prime suspect, Musando, had on Monday, July 9, 2018, denied charges of trafficking 674.7 grams of heroin with a market value of KSh 1,943,100 by importation using hardcover books, and was released on a bond of KSh 1 million with a similar surety.
