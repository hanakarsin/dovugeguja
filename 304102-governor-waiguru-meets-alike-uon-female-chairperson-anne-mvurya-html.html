Governor Waiguru meets her look-alike UON first female chairperson Anne Mvurya

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Sunday, July 21, 2024

Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru and University of Nairobi Student Association's (UNSA) first female chairperson Ann Mvurya have finally met after weeks of speculation over their striking resemblance.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, the student leader hinted at meeting Waiguru to establish if they were related by any chance.

Through her Twitter handle on Thursday, April 25, Waiguru demonstrated her admiration for the young ambitious student leader after hosting her in her office where they discussed various issues including women in leadership.

"Had an intriguing meeting newly elected UNSA chair, @MwangiMvurya. We share quite abit in common including a shared passion for more women in political leadership," tweeted Waiguru

The 24-year-old law student who was accompanied by other UNSA leaders presented the governor with a portrait.

Waiguru, who was also stunned by the resemblance had promised to meet the young Mvurya and has been following her closely ever since she was elected.

The county boss was among the hundreds of Kenyans who congratulated the young politician for her win in a male dominated contest.

Many Kenyans have been asking questions about the spitting look-alike features between the women leaders and have been anticipating their meeting for quite some time.
