Kenyan newspaper review for March 8: Uhuru's alleged plot to hand over power to Mudavadi irks Raila'

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Thursday, May 23, 2024

On Monday, March 8, Kenyan newspapers reported extensively on the 2022 succession politics and the suspected fallout between President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga.

The dailies also reported on the COVID-19 vaccine after the exercise was rolled out in the country on Friday, March 5.

The Standard

The Opposition chief cancelled his much-hyped rally at the famous Kamukunji ground in Kibra constituency in Nairobi at the last minute amid a report of a phone call from the head of state.

The president reportedly reached out to Raila hours after his allies, Siaya senator James Orengo and Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo threatened that ODM could bolt out of the working arrangements with Jubilee due to alleged interference by powerful individuals at the Office of the President.

The daily reported that the remarks by the two lawmakers had the blessings of Raila following claims that a Cabinet Secretary and Interior Principal Secretary Karanja Kibicho were trying to meddle with the Building Bridges Initiative.

There are fears that state mandarins were favouring one side of the handshake team and maybe bidding their time with Raila.

Daily Nation

Details of a plot by senior government officials to influence Uhuru's 2022 succession continued to emerge on Sunday, March 7, with Gideon Moi and Musalia Mudavadi said to be favoured by the state-operatives.

While the plan is said to be in its nascent stages, allies of Raila have protested the plot they see as undermining the March 2018, handshake between the former prime minister and the head of state.

Coming barely three months after the president hinted at a rotational presidency that would have locked out senator Moi and Deputy President William Ruto to both of whom are from the Kalenjin community alongside Kikuyu politicians, the plan is said to be picking up the pace.

Both Moi and Mudavadi are said to have the ears of the Kenyatta family and are seen as the safest bets to take over after the president.

People Daily

The second in command dared the so-called 'super alliance coalition' to duel in next year's polls saying he will win the election in the morning.

Making reference to the pact signed by Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka, KANU's Gideon Moi, ANC's Mudavadi and FORD Kenya's Moses Wetangula, he said the group was incapable of leading the country.

Ruto, who was on a three-day visit to the Meru region said he is not afraid of the political grouping and dismissed the group as joyriders.

The Star

World Health Organisation (WHO) has dismissed claims by some local doctors affiliated with the Catholic church that the COVID-19 vaccine could be unsafe.

The organisation's Nairobi office said the claims are untrue.

"Our attention is drawn to the falsehood and uncertainties being circulated about this vaccine as unnecessary, questionable or unsafe," it said in a statement.

The statement said that WHO has affirmed that the Astra Zeneca vaccine produced by India's Serum Institute and being distributed in Kenya has been reviewed and found to be safe.

Taifa Leo

The daily reported that KANU has insisted its chairman Gideon Moi will succeed Uhuru in 2022.

Tiaty MP William Kamket said Moi will join hands with Mudavadi, Wetangula, Kalonzo and Mombasa governor Hassan Joho.

However, Joho had earlier disclosed that he will support Raila's bid should he be floored in the outfit's nominations.
