Former IEBC chief executive Ezra Chiloba appointed as Youth Fund board member

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Tuesday, February 6, 2024

- Ezra Chiloba was appointed to the board by ICT Cabinet Secretary Joe Mucheru

- The embattled ex-CEO is expected to serve for three years

-Chiloba unceremoniously exited the electoral body in September 2018

Former Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Ezra Chiloba has landed another government job.

Chiloba was appointed a member of the Youth Enterprise Development Fund (YEDF) Board by Information Communication and Technology (ICT) Cabinet Secretary Joe Mucheru.

According to a Gazette notice dated Thursday, December 31, the embattled ex-CEO will serve in the board for three years.

"In exercise of the powers conferred by paragraph 5 (2) (e) (i) of the Youth Enterprise Development Fund Order, 2007, the Cabinet Secretary for ICT, Innovation and Youth Affairs appoints Ezra Chiloba to be a member of the Youth Enterprise Development Fund Board, for a period of three (3) years," said Mucheru.

Youth Fund

The Youth Enterprise Development Fund is a state corporation under the Ministry of ICT, Innovation and Youth Affairs.

The Fund seeks to create employment opportunities for young people through entrepreneurship and encouraging them to be job creators and not job seekers.

It does this by providing easy and affordable financial and business development support services to youth who are keen on starting or expanding businesses.

Fired from IEBC

Chiloba unceremoniously exited the electoral body in September 2018.

He had been sent on compulsory leave to pave way for an audit to trace misappropriation of funds in the 2017 General Election

Later, he announced that he had been maliciously kicked out of the commission by Chairman Wafula Chebukati.

"My employment with IEBC has officially been terminated. This doesn't come as a surprise to Kenyans who have been monitoring events at the IEBC in the last six months. The outcome was already predetermined and the process was a mere formality," he said.

Chiloba, however, decried reasons raised by the chairman in sacking him, citing malice.

According to him, the letter stated he had twice snubbed disciplinary committee summons hence warranting his unconditional dismissal despite him informing the commission he was attending classes at the University of Oxford.
