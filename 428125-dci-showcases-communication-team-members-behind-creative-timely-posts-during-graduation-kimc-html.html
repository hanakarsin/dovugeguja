DCI Showcases Communication Team Members Behind Creative and Timely Posts During Graduation from KIM

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Wednesday, May 1, 2024
  • The identities of the DCI communication team have never been revealed to the public to be appreciated for their exemplary work
  • The team was unveiled during their graduation from the Kenya Institute of Mass Communication (KIMC) on Thursday, September 30
  • The ceremony presided over by the DCI boss George Kinoti was a culmination of an intensive media training programme

They have kept Kenyans updated on social media platforms with their creative and timely posts on crime-related incidents for a long time now.

However, their identities have never been revealed to the public to be appreciated for their exemplary work.

DCI team unveiled

But it was until their graduation on Thursday, September 30, that the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) detectives behind the social media posts were made known to the public.

The eight detectives based at DCI's Corporate Communication and Public Affairs unit were conferred with certificates in advanced media production at a colourful ceremony held at the Kenya Institute of Mass Communication (KIMC) in Nairobi.

The ceremony presided over by the DCI boss George Kinoti was a culmination of an intensive media training programme that was specifically tailored to fit the needs and requirements of the DCI in communicating to the people we serve.

The course offered at the media training institution aimed to expose the detectives to the latest media technological trends to align them with the contemporary communication demands of time.

Communication as an essential tool in fighting crime

Addressing the detectives, Kinoti noted that communication was at the core of their work.

He observed that it had been the most excellent tool lacking in the country's crime management for a long time.

"I must say that this is the first time that the National Police Service (NPS) and the DCI in particular has offered professional training to police journalists and it exemplifies the importance that DCI attaches to effective communication, in order to bridge the gap that has for a long time existed between us and the public," said Kinoti.

Being the brainchild behind the unit's formation in the investigations agency, the DCI chief, the National Police Service spokesperson before his current appointment, added that communication is crucial like any other science such as architecture, medicine, and engineering discipline in the increasingly globalized world.

On his part, the institution's director Hiram Mucheke lauded the Directorate for appreciating the need to train communication practitioners within its ranks.

''From my many years of experience as a journalist, police and the media have not been known to enjoy cordial relations. I, therefore, welcome the move to train more police journalists and commend Kinoti for giving the state agency a human face,'' Mucheke said.

Mucheke challenged the officers to practice the skills they had acquired to assist the Directorate in improving the public safety and security services it offers to Kenyans.
