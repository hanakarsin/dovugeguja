Eldoret Cleric Wants Town Installed with Tractor, Maize Stalks and Athletes Sculptures Landmarks

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Friday, June 14, 2024

- According to Daniel Rugut, the town lacks an easy to the eye landmark that can remind visitors where they are once at the centre of Uasin Gishu county

- The Eldoret pastor said the town is known as the country’s breadbasket city and deserves a landmark

- Rugut said the landmark will open doors for investors into the region and the county will develop

Most, if not all, popular towns in Kenya have that one thing that you can easily identify them with.

With the dispensation of devolution, county governments were deliberate to coin slogans and trademarks that would distinguish them from others in a positive light.

The battle ended after Uasin Gishu settled for City of Champions, Elgeyo Marakwet (Home of Champions), and Nandi adopted the Source of Champions slogan, which are usually portrayed at entry points or emblazoned on county headquarters offices.

Daniel Rugut, Eldoret-based pastor, however, argued that the town lacks an easy to the eye landmark that would remind visitors that they are at the centre of Uasin Gishu county.

Rugut who doubles up as Academic Dean Reformed Institute for Theological Traininghas already visualised the town having a gigantic tractor sculpture or an old tractor adjacent to maize stalks in every entry point to Eldoret and Uasin Gishu.

According to the man of the cloth, the region has been without any reference point for a long time despite being the gateway to the Northern Corridor that snakes all the way to Uganda.

“In any city around the world, there is always a mark that makes it describable among visitors and investors. The same should be done to our town, which carries a rich history dating from the colonial times,” Rugut told TUKO.co.ke.

He urged the county assembly and the Executive to ensure the town has a conspicuous point of reference that would also be of aesthetic value.

“The county government should form a board to come up with guidelines of installing an old tractor, maize stalks and milk churn, which will highlight the economic activities of this city. They should consider putting athletes’ sculptures in strategic points across the town,” he stated.

Rugut noted that this would signify appreciating the farmers’ hard work and athletes’ stellar performances in global tracks and roads.

The man of God was categorical that having a reference mark in town would also open doors for investors keen on setting up facilities and industries in the agriculture rich region besides improving the scenery as a tourist attraction area.

The town has been undergoing facelifts courtesy of World Bank and county government-funded projects such as footpaths that have made life easier for pedestrians in town and several estates.

His sentiments come when Kisumu city is being spruced up ahead of the upcoming Madaraka Day celebrations on June 1.

Among other facelifts, Kisumu has seen additional gigantic fish and boat sculptures erected in the Central Busines District, with footpaths being upgraded ahead of President Uhuru Kenyatta’s tour to a region regarded Opposition Leader Raila Odinga’s backyard.

The facelifts have earned Raila, Uhuru working partnership praise from most Luo Nyanza region residents.
