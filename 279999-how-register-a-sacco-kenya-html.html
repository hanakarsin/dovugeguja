How to register a SACCO society in Kenya

Before a SACCO starts to engage in any deposit taking business, it must first acquire a valid license from the Authority. The SACCO must first obtain a license in order to start the deposit taking business after filing an application to the Authority while fulfilling the below SACCO registration regulations.

Licensing requirements

The documents that must be submitted include:

  • A duly filled and completed application filed together with the “fit and proper test” as set out in the Forms 1 and 2 in the first schedule of the regulations.
  • A certified copy of the SACCO Society’s registration certificate that have been issued under the Co-operative Societies Act (Cap.490)
  • A certified copy of the SACCO Society’s registration certificate that have been issued under the Co-operative Societies Act (Cap.490).
  • A certified copy of the SACCO society’s bylaws.
  • A three-year business plan together with a feasibility study of the SACCO society in order to be licensed with the detailed vision, mission, scope and the nature of the business operations, the projected profitability in order to achieve the set standards, monitoring procedures and the control measures.
  • A certified extract of the minutes after a general meeting resolution authorizing the licensing of the SACCO by the members.
  • A name of the newly elected Chief Executive Officer.
  • List of the certified copies of the financial statements for the past three years, where applicable.
  • Evidence that the SACCO has adequate capital to run.

How to register a youth SACCO in Kenya

SACCO registration for non-deposit taking

Here is a step by step guide to help in registering your youth SACCO:

  • Formulate a formal request in a letter prescribing the need for the formation of the youth SACCO.
  • Provide the names of the members for search and approval.
  • Formulate the objectives of the society.
  • Provide the multitude of members that would be in the SACCO.
  • Propose the names, occupational standards, home and postal addresses of the chairman, secretary, and treasurer that form part of the committee.
  • Formulate the proposed physical address of the society. The address also includes the road, plot number, town and county.
  • Form the constitution of the SACCO.
  • Fill form A which is the application for the registration of the SACCO and form B which is the notification of the registered office and the address of the society.

Minimum capital requirements for operating a SACCO

At all times of the operation of the SACCO, the following must be maintained throughout:

  • The core capital should not be less than ten million shillings
  • The core capital should not be less than 10% of the total assets
  • The institutional capital should not be less than 8% of the total assets
  • The core capital should not be less than 8% of the total deposits made to the SACCO.

Before registering a SACCO, you should first counter check to see that it does not engage in the following activities:

  • Foreign trade operations
  • Trust operations
  • Investment in capital beyond the prescribed limit
  • Transacting SACCO society business with non-members
  • Purchase and acquiring of any land except as one that can be seen as an attempt to expand the operation and coverage of the SACCO business beyond the prescribed limits.

After the registration of the SACCO, there is also an inspection of the SACCO societies. The Authority is responsible for the supervision of the SACCO societies to ensure that they comply with the provisions of the Act. Here are some of the regulations, their bylaws, policies, procedures and other applicable law. The Authority is allowed by the law to have unlimited access to all the premises and records of the SACCO society. Also, the Authority may at any point require the SACCO society to furnish the records that it may deem necessary and in the form in which it prescribes.

Some of the areas that the Authority would be interested in during the assessment of the SACCO society include the:

  • Checking on the compliance to the capital adequacy requirements
  • Checking on the composition of the assets, liabilities and the equity accounts
  • Oversight of the quality of the earning assets
  • The financial, operational and business risks

In case the authority is dissatisfied with the reports, it may demand for additional information from the SACCO or make an impromptu on-site inspection. In case of an on-site assessment, a report shall be drafted which draws attention to the breach and non-observance of the requirements of the Act and the regulations. The report contains an analysis of the SACCO which highlights the performance of the SACCO society and the set standards.

Once all the steps have been adhered to by the SACCO society, they will be offered with a Certificate of Registration from the Registrar. This will enable the SACCO society to gain a SACCO bank account, other associate business permits, NHIF and NSSF, and other tax arrangements.
